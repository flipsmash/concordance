The Reading Room

A susurrus moved through the lugubrious hall as Aragorn entered. The old
librarian, a lugubrious man of obfuscating habits, peered over his spectacles.
He was wont to obfuscate the simplest request, wrapping it in perorations no
one asked for. Definately, the boy thought, this was a mistake.

Motes of dust hung in the crepuscular light. She felt a sudden apophenia,
seeing patterns in the marbled endpapers, in the susurrus of turning pages, in
the very grain of the desks. The word susurrus pleased her; she repeated it.

Aragorn set down a heavy quarto bound in shagreen, its pages foxed and brittle.
"A perspicacious choice," murmured the librarian, and for once he did not
obfuscate. The clock ticked. The rain came down. It was an ordinary afternoon
in an ordinary town, and yet the room felt numinous, hushed, and strange.
